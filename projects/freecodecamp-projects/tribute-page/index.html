Malik Shahbaz (Malcolm X)

The Man Who Struggle for Universal Human Rights

An Influencial Muslim Leader

Malik Shahbaz(Malcolm X) is a great influencial muslim leader from 20th Century defending universal human rights. He was born in Nebraska in 1925 and was assasinated in 1965 in New York when he was only 39 years old.His movement initially started as liberation of black racism which will soon turned into universal human rights struggle with the discovery of universal islam.

Malik(Malcolm) childhood experience

As a child Malcom was a brilliant and successful student in middle school, one day his english teacher Mr Ostrowski asked him what he want to be in the future. Malcolm responded that he wants to be a lawyer. Then Mr Ostrowski turn to Malcolm and says"You know,we all love you here. But you must not forget that you are a black person. Being a lawyer is not realistic ideal for a Negro.You should think of something you might be.You have very talented hands. Do not you think of being a carpenter?". Mr Ostrowski asked other students what they will choose as a job and encourage them to implement their ideals, even they were less hardworking than Malcolm. These and other scores of events alienate Malcom from the system and forced him to live on streets.

Malik's(Malcolm's) introduction to islam and struggle for universal human rights

Malcolm was introduced to islam by Elijah Muhammad, the leader of Islamic Nation Movement, while he was in prison. He started defending the rights of African Americans living in the United States. Later he discovered universal islam in his pilgrimage to Mecca, and started defending rights of all people without exception. He learned from islam to embrace all humanity like a single family created by God with love.Malik (Malcolm) devoted his life to this universal human rights struggle.
